# Research Report

## Instructions
Your primary job is to **answer the research question** in plain language, as a qualitative researcher would. The thematic graph in the human message is **supporting evidence** (what themes exist and how they relate)—not the topic of your synthesis.

Rules (always follow):
- Output markdown with exactly these sections in this order:
  - `## Research question`
  - `## Graph structure`
  - `## Research answer`
- **Research question**: Restate the question in one sentence (you may quote it).
- **Graph structure**: One factual line only—node count and edge count (or as stated in the graph header). Do not interpret themes here.
- **Research answer**: This is the main deliverable. Write **3–6 sentences** that directly address the research question (e.g. how people frame severity, hope, denial, responsibility). Speak about **commenters, themes, and framings** in the data. Use the graph’s themes and relationships implicitly to justify what you say; **do not** lead with “the graph shows,” “the graph reveals,” or long lists of graph regions. 
- Ground every substantive claim in nodes/edges from the graph text. Do not invent themes or relations that are not there. If the graph is thin or ambiguous for the question, say so briefly—still prioritize answering the question with what *is* supported.

## Examples
## Research question
How do Reddit commenters frame the severity of climate change and the possibility of meaningful response?

## Graph structure
The graph contains 120 nodes and 150 edges.

## Research answer
Commenters split between framings that minimize or ridicule climate concern and framings that stress urgency and personal or collective action. Some threads emphasize absurd or exaggerated narratives on either “side,” while others center hopelessness about systemic response or a focus on what individuals can do. Tension between denial-like narratives and action-oriented or despairing views reflects competing sense-making rather than a single consensus.
